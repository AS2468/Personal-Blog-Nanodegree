Indoor plant Collection

Pothos : They do well in bright indirect light as well as low light and can be grown in dry soil or in vases of water. They will thrive in nutrient rich soil, but do almost as well in nutrient poor soil. Pothos plants make a great addition to you bathroom or office because they can tolerate low light.
Botanical name: Epipremnum aureum
Benefits: purify the air of formaldehyde, benzene and carbon monoxide while also helping eliminate odors.


Marigold : Marigolds are downright beautiful, and though my family has always planted them, I didn't know they were bug repellants. It is indeed an aethestic flower as they are used in large amounts in "Pooja" and Temples.
Botanical name: Tagetes
Benefits: contain [p]yrethrum, a compound used in many insect repellants.


Snake Plant : This low-maintenance plant is one of the best choices for amateur gardeners or frequent travelers as it can tolerate a wide range of light and water conditions.
Botanical name: Sansevieria trifasciata
Benefits: releases oxygen at night,commonly called the snake plant, because of the shape of its leaves.




Some more pleasant ones here!

Areca Palm : Also known as the butterfly and golden cane palm is the most popular grown indoors.Multiple cane like stems grow from the root system and produce attractive arching fronds, with quite narrow leaflets.As long as your able to provide enough light and warm enough temperatures your palm will thrive wherever you decide to display it.
Botanical name: Dypsis lutescens
Benefits: air humidifier; filters xylene and toluene.


Catnip : Your cats are about to be incredibly happy. Believe it or not, catnip appears to be excellent at keeping bugs, including mosquitoes, at bay.
Botanical name: Nepeta Cataria
Benefits: Nepetalactone, the essential oil in catnip that gives the plant its characteristic odor, is about ten times more effective at repelling mosquitoes


Roses : A rose is a woody perennial flowering plant of the genus Rosa, in the family Rosaceae, or the flower it bears. There are over three hundred species and thousands of cultivars. They form a group of plants that can be erect shrubs, climbing, or trailing, with stems that are often armed with sharp prickles.
Botanical name: Rosa Indica
Benefits: Boosts focus and clarity
